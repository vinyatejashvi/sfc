What Are Functional Foods?

Functional foods are foods that have a potentially positive effect on health beyond basic nutrition. They are not pills or supplements, but part of a normal food pattern, consumed to promote health and reduce disease risk. We can categorize them into three main types based on their preparation.

Unlike other carbohydrates, NDCs are not broken down in the small intestine. They travel to the large intestine where they become food for our beneficial gut bacteria. This process of fermentation is key to their health benefits.

Focus on Dietary Fibre

Dietary fibre is a well-known NDC primarily found in plant-based foods. It's broadly classified into two types based on its solubility in water, each offering distinct health benefits.

Focus on Resistant Starch (RS)

Resistant Starch acts similarly to fibre. It's a type of starch that resists digestion and is categorized into five types based on its physical form and source.

Focus on Oligosaccharides

Oligosaccharides are a key type of NDC, known for their prebiotic effects. They are short chains of sugar molecules that selectively feed beneficial gut bacteria.

The Engine of Health: SCFA Production

When gut bacteria ferment NDCs, they produce Short-Chain Fatty Acids (SCFAs). These molecules are powerhouses, providing energy to our colon cells and influencing overall health in numerous positive ways.

Butyrate

The primary fuel source for colon cells, supporting gut barrier integrity and possessing anti-inflammatory properties.

Propionate

Helps regulate blood sugar by contributing to glucose production in the liver and may help reduce cholesterol levels.

Acetate

The most abundant SCFA, it plays a role in central appetite regulation and serves as a substrate for fat synthesis.
